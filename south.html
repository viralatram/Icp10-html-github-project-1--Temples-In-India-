Famous Temples of South India

  • Meenakshi Temple, Madurai
  • Brihadeeswarar Temple, Thanjavur
  • Shore Temple, Mamallapuram
  • Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Thiruvananthapuram
  • Venkateswara Temple, Tirupati
  • Ramanathaswamy Temple, Rameswaram

Meenakshi Temple, Madurai

Deity: Dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (an incarnation of Parvati) and Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva).
Architecture: Dravidian style, renowned for its tall gopurams (gateway towers) and intricate sculptures.
Wealth: Considered one of the most culturally rich temples, with significant donations from devotees.
History: Built during the 6th century by the Pandya dynasty, with expansions by the Nayak rulers in the 16th century.

Brihadeeswarar Temple, Thanjavur

Deity: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Brihadeeswarar.
Architecture: A masterpiece of Chola architecture, famous for its towering Vimana (temple tower), standing at 216 feet.
Wealth: Known for its historical significance rather than material wealth, reflecting the grandeur of the Chola Empire.
History: Built in the 11th century by Raja Raja Chola I, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of Chola dynasty's architectural excellence.

Shore Temple, Mamallapuram

Deity: Dedicated to Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu.
Architecture: Built in the Dravidian style, it consists of two shrines facing the sea, made from granite.
Wealth: Known for its cultural and architectural significance, not for material wealth.
History: Constructed in the 8th century by the Pallava dynasty under King Narasimhavarman II, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Thiruvananthapuram

Deity: Dedicated to Lord Vishnu in a reclining posture on the serpent Anantha (Adi Shesha)
Architecture: Combines Dravidian and Kerala architectural styles, featuring intricate carvings and sculptures.
Wealth: Known as one of the richest temples in the world, with vast treasures discovered in its secret vaults.
History: Believed to be over 1,000 years old, with mentions in ancient texts like the Bhagavata Purana.

Venkateswara Temple, Tirupati

Deity: Dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, also known as Balaji.
Architecture: Dravidian style, with a golden gopuram (tower) and intricately designed mandapams.
Wealth: Known as one of the richest temples in the world, with substantial offerings from devotees.
History: Believed to date back to the 8th century, with legends linking it to the Vaishnavism tradition.

Ramanathaswamy Temple, Rameswaram

Deity: Dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of Ramanathaswamy (Shiva Lingam).
Architecture: Famous for its Dravidian architecture, particularly its long corridor with elaborately carved pillars.
Wealth: Holds religious significance but is known more for its spiritual importance than material wealth.
History: Linked to the Ramayana, where Lord Rama is said to have worshipped Shiva here after defeating Ravana.
